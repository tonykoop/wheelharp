# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

Photorealistic studio product photograph of a Wheelharp-style controlled-friction keyboard instrument as a finished prototype: a sweeping semicircular keyboard arcs across the front, and rising behind it sits a curved bank of small rosined bowing wheels, each presenting a true-running edge to its assigned string. Strings route from the keyboard up into the wheel bank so that pressing a key engages one string against its spinning wheel, with separate felt dampers poised between strings for clean release. Show guarded wheel rims, a quiet enclosed motor-drive housing along the base, removable key-action modules, accessible service panels, and a refined frame implied in warm hardwood with brushed-metal axle supports and fittings. Scale reads as a substantial tabletop or console instrument a seated player could reach across. Clean neutral studio background, soft directional studio light, 3/4 product-photography angle, approximately 50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

Photorealistic workshop scene: a maker bench holds the Wheelharp one-wheel coupon and a removable key-action mule staged beside the larger semicircular instrument concept. A hand presses a single key while one string contacts a rosined wheel and its damper lifts in sequence, capturing the note-on path. A rosin cloth, a wheel-truing tool, a small motor guard, and a handwritten service checklist sit nearby on the bench. Natural window light, shallow depth of field, honest engineering-prototype atmosphere, environmental storytelling, concept visualization only.

## Detail Prompt

Photorealistic macro close-up of the Wheelharp note-on mechanism: a slender key lever drawing one string toward the rosined edge of a continuously turning wheel, a felt damper pad lifting just before contact, the guarded wheel rim showing fine rosin texture, and a small adjustment screw governing contact pressure. Raking light grazes the wheel edge and string, revealing wood grain, felt nap, brushed metal, and the rosin film. High detail, no measurements.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension callouts or measurement lines; no piano hammers, unguarded spinning parts near fingers, impossible wheel/string overlap, or fantasy keyboard layouts; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
